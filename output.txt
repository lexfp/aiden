DESIGN DECISIONS
----------------

1. BASELINE HP SCALING (health / 130)
   The JJK_CHARACTER_TYPES define health as raw values (100–200), but the actual bot HP
   must scale with difficulty. Using 130 as the baseline (midpoint of the range) ensures
   Sukuna (200) spawns with ~53% more HP than the difficulty baseline, while Mahito (100)
   spawns with ~23% less. This keeps the game balanced across Easy/Hard/Extreme difficulty.

2. ABILITY COOLDOWNS START STAGGERED
   Initial abilityCooldown is set to 2 + random*4 seconds (not 0). If all bots fired their
   abilities instantly on spawn, the player would take massive burst damage right at match
   start. Staggering prevents this while keeping abilities active early in the match.

3. SUMMON (MEGUMI) — WIN CONDITION EXCLUSION
   Summoned shikigami are flagged with isSummon=true. The "all enemies dead" win condition
   now checks: every(b => !b.alive || b.isSummon). This means killing all real enemies
   wins the match even if summons are still alive. The enemy counter in the HUD also
   excludes summons so the player knows the true objective count.

4. GETO PROJECTILE — DELAYED DAMAGE
   Rather than building a full projectile movement system (which would require adding
   projectile objects to the render loop), Geto's attack fires a tracer immediately and
   checks player position 0.8 seconds later with a setTimeout. The player must MOVE to
   dodge it — if they're within 2.5 units of where they were when shot, they take 70 dmg.
   This simulates a slow projectile cleanly without a new game loop subsystem.

5. SHIELD COLOR RESTORE
   When Gojo's shield expires, it re-sets all mesh colors to this.color (the bot's JJK
   color). This is safe because the bot's color is stored on the instance and never changes.

6. EMISSIVE GLOW VIA POINT LIGHT
   Instead of modifying material emissive values (which varies by mesh and material type),
   strong enemies (Sukuna, Yuta, Toji) get a THREE.PointLight added to their group. This
   casts colored light onto nearby geometry and is visually distinct regardless of whether
   the bot is using a GLTF soldier model or the fallback boxy mesh.

OBSERVATIONS ABOUT THE GAME
----------------------------

Strengths:
  - Procedural audio synthesis is clever — no audio files, works offline, each weapon
    sounds distinct. This is the most underappreciated system in the game.
  - The weapon variety (50+ weapons across 6 categories) gives real replay value and the
    tier/pricing system creates a genuine progression loop.
  - Bot obstacle avoidance with 10 steering angles is simple but effective. The bots don't
    get perfectly stuck and navigate around cover reasonably well.
  - Building the entire 3D game as a single .html + .js file (no build step) is impressive
    and makes deployment trivially simple.

Areas for future improvement (not implemented — listed for reference only):
  - The bot pathfinding has no memory of where it came from, so bots can oscillate between
    two points when blocked. A simple visited-direction penalty would fix this.
  - Survival mode wave scaling (hp * 1.34 per wave, acc + 0.04) makes wave 5 nearly
    impossible on Extreme difficulty. A gentler curve or a hard cap on acc/hp would
    improve balance.
  - The "timed hunt" mode's bot respawn timer (4 seconds) is too long — experienced
    players can kill bots faster than they respawn, making the mode feel stale. 2 seconds
    would be better.
  - No visual feedback for the player's HP regeneration or critical HP state (besides the
    berserker rage text). A pulsing red vignette below 20% HP would improve readability.

JJK ABILITY SYSTEM SUMMARY
---------------------------
Gojo   (shield)     — 3s invulnerability every 12s. All damage blocked.
Sukuna (slash)      — 40 AoE damage within 6 units every 5s.
Nanami (critical)   — Next shot deals 3x damage every 8s.
Mahito (dodge)      — 40% chance to ignore hits for 2s every 10s.
Megumi (summon)     — Spawns 2 small shikigami (Mahito-type, 35% HP, 0.6 scale) every 15s.
Yuji   (burst)      — 1.8x speed + 1.5x shot damage for 4s every 9s.
Yuta   (energy)     — 40 dmg ranged attack bypassing weapon range every 7s.
Toji   (rush)       — Dashes 4 units toward player, deals 50 melee dmg if within 3 units.
Geto   (projectile) — 70 dmg slow projectile (delayed hit check at 0.8s) every 8s.

All abilities respect existing systems: they call G.playerTakeDamage(), spawnImpact(),
spawnTracer(), and particles — no new rendering paths introduced.
